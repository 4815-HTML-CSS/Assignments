Types of tabletop games

Tabletop games can be classified according to the general form, or equipment utilized.

Types of Tabletop Games
Category Definition Game examples
Board games any game played on a board, especially one that involves the movement of pieces on the board, such as chess or checkers. Adventure board games, adventure gamebooks, backgammon, chess, German-style board games, go, reversi
Card games any game using playing cards as the primary device with which the game is played, be they traditional or game-specific. solitaire, collectible card games, hanafuda, tarot card games, poker
Dice games games that use or incorporate one or more dice as their sole or central component, usually as a random device. bunco, craps, poker dice, sic bo, yahtzee
Tabletop role-playing games a game played around a table or online in which a group of people create and play the part of characters in a story that develops as the game is played. Dungeons & Dragons, Pathfinder Roleplaying Game
Strategy games any game in which the players' uncoerced, and often autonomous, decision-making skills have a high significance in determining the outcome. wargames, government simulation games, miniatures games
Paper and pencil games games that can be played solely with paper and pencils (or other writing implements), usually without erasing. Battleship, Connect 5, dots and boxes, hangman, sprouts, sudoku

Tabletop game examples

Tabletop games or tabletops are games that are normally played on a table or other flat surface, such as board games, card games, dice games, miniature wargames, or tile-based games.

Games like chess and draughts are examples of games belonging to the board game category. Other games, however, use various attributes and cannot be classified unambiguously (e.g. Monopoly utilises a board as well as dice and cards).

You will find some examples of some table top games below.

Board Games

Ticket to Ride

Ticket to Ride London

Ticket to Ride is a railway-themed German-style board game. The game's original version is played on a board depicting a railway map of the United States and southern Canada. Localized editions have subsequently been published depicting maps of other countries, cities, and regions.

Catan

The Settlers of Catan

Players take on the roles of settlers, each attempting to build and develop holdings while trading and acquiring resources. Players gain victory points as their settlements grow and the first to reach a set number of victory points, typically 10, wins.
